Poems by Eric Entsminger

Pounds

One more time
Makes a happy little me.
Just three pounds to go,
Over there,
Hold it there,

Hold it there.

Photoshopped crop tops,
They're staring at me.
Three turns to
Eight and I
Wait to consume,

Hours.

Eight turns to
Five and I
Still don't want to be alive,
Tell me.
Why am I
Trapped in this
Shell?
My own suburban hell where
Energy is the enemy.

My teeth rot,
And my heart stops.


To the Sons and Daughters

Did her laugh make you shudder?
Did her smile make you meek?

Did her palm across your shoulder
Make the world seem less bleak?

Did her hair fall down like marigolds,
And fall out in the end?
Did life ever prepare you for that monumental bend?

Into
Night,
Into Darkness.
Where we all are meant to go.
Did those final moments raze you
When you knew it would be so?

How long before you heal?
If healing ever comes.

How long before her name might
Tenderly escape your lungs?
